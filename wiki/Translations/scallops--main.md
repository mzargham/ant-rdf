# Translation: The Callon-1986 translation chain

[← Home](../Home) · [Case: scallops](../Cases/scallops)

<!-- https://w3id.org/ant/cases/scallops/translation/main -->

The translation that Callon traces from problematization through mobilization. Ultimately precarious: a single winter's trawling unravels the web (Law 2008 quotation: 'all it takes is for one translation to fail and the whole web of reality unravels').

## Moments (in canonical order)

### Problematization

**Problematization: declining scallop populations**

<!-- https://w3id.org/ant/cases/scallops/moment/problematization -->

The three researchers define the problem: St Brieuc Bay scallop populations are declining and unstudied. They position themselves as indispensable by proposing the larvae-collector experiment and positioning fishermen and scallops as needing to pass through their experimental apparatus.

### Interessement

**Interessement: locking in roles**

<!-- https://w3id.org/ant/cases/scallops/moment/interessement -->

The researchers cut competing alternatives — they negotiate with fishermen to refrain from trawling protected zones, and they design collectors that prevent scallop larvae from anchoring anywhere else. Each actant is interested into a single role within the network.

### Enrolment

**Enrolment: coordinated roles**

<!-- https://w3id.org/ant/cases/scallops/moment/enrolment -->

Scallops successfully anchor in significant numbers (some seasons). Fishermen accept the no-trawl zones. Scientific colleagues accept the experimental results. Each actant plays its scripted role.

### Mobilization

**Mobilization: spokespersons emerge**

<!-- https://w3id.org/ant/cases/scallops/moment/mobilization -->

The researchers speak for the scallops (citing anchoring counts), for the fishermen (citing community acceptance), and for the bay itself (citing the technique's promise). The network speaks with one voice — until it doesn't.
