## Network — The hotel-keys assemblage

<!-- https://w3id.org/ant/cases/hotel-keys/network -->

The network of hotel manager, guests, front-desk staff, key, weighted fob, and request sign analyzed by Latour (1991) in 'Technology is society made durable'. The fob translates the moral injunction 'please return your key' into a physical inconvenience that achieves what the polite sign alone cannot. Reproduces Goodwin & Kuehn (2021) 'Latour's Hotel Keys: An Actor-Network Ontology' (NASKO 8) using the synthesized ant-rdf vocabulary.

## Translations

### [The hotel-keys translation chain](Translation-hotel-keys--main)

Latour's classical demonstration that technologies act — the heavy fob enrols the guest's body where the polite sign could not. Reads through all four Callon moments.

**Problematization.** Problematization: guests leave with keys — The manager problematizes: 'guests are leaving with the keys, and we need them back to turn over rooms'.

**Interessement.** Interessement: try a sign, then try a fob — The manager tries to interest guests with a polite sign. Sign fails (Latour 1991's key empirical finding). Manager swaps in a heavy fob, cutting off the alternative of pocketing the key comfortably.

**Enrolment.** Enrolment: guests start returning keys — Guests, finding the weighted key uncomfortable in pocket or bag, increasingly return it to the desk on the way out. The fob has enrolled the guest's body into the manager's program.

**Mobilization.** Mobilization: the fob speaks for the manager — Even when the manager is asleep, the fob continues to enforce the program. Latour's point: the network's voice is delegated to a non-human spokesperson that never gets tired.

## Actants in this case

- **[Weighted brass fob](Actant-hotel-keys--fob)** — The heavy metal weight attached to the key. The material delegate that translates the manager's moral program into physical inconvenience. Latour's central exhibit: the missing mass that closes the ci…
- **[Front-desk staff](Actant-hotel-keys--front-desk)** — The hotel staff who receive returned keys and notice missing ones.
- **[Hotel guest](Actant-hotel-keys--guest)** — The guest occupying a room. Has an anti-program (taking the key with them, intentionally or by forgetting it).
- **[The hotel building](Actant-hotel-keys--hotel)** — The infrastructural actant whose continued operation depends on keys being available for room turnover.
- **[Room key](Actant-hotel-keys--key)** — The mechanical key itself that opens the door. Without delegation, has no agency over guest behaviour.
- **[Hotel manager](Actant-hotel-keys--manager)** — The hotel's manager who originally posts the sign requesting key returns. Problematizer of the network: defines guests leaving with keys as the problem.
- **[Polite request sign](Actant-hotel-keys--sign)** — A sign in the room asking guests to leave their key at the front desk. The pre-delegation inscription. Latour's foil — proof that words alone are insufficient.

## Programs of action

- **Return keys to front desk before leaving**
  > The manager's program of action: guests must hand their key to the front desk when they leave the building. Originally inscribed only in the polite sign.
  - opposes: *Take key with you (or forget at home)*
- **Take key with you (or forget at home)**
  > The guest's anti-program: take the key wherever you're going, in pocket or bag, intentionally or by forgetting it on the night table. The empirically dominant program before the fob's introduction.
  - opposes: *Return keys to front desk before leaving*
