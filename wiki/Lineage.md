# Lineage — intellectual and technical

[← Home](Home.md) · [← About](About.md)

The vocabulary `ant-rdf` codifies, and the methodological discipline it enacts, both inherit from explicit predecessors. Two lineages run in parallel: the *intellectual* lineage of material-semiotic / Actor-Network thought that the ontology synthesises term-by-term, and the *technical* lineage of the docs-as-code pattern this repository instantiates. Both are named here because both are load-bearing.

### Intellectual lineage — material-semiotics / Actor-Network Theory

Every term in [`ontology/material-semiotics-core.ttl`](https://github.com/mzargham/ant-rdf/blob/main/ontology/material-semiotics-core.ttl) carries a `dcterms:source` field naming the founding-text move it inherits. The synthesis pulls from four primary canons plus the one published ANT-in-RDF precedent:

- **Michel Callon — sociology of translation.** Callon, M. (1986). *Some Elements of a Sociology of Translation: Domestication of the Scallops and the Fishermen of St Brieuc Bay*; Callon, M. (1984). The four moments — **problematization, interessement, enrolment, mobilization** — form the procedural backbone of `ant:Translation`; the **obligatory passage point** (OPP) and the principle of **generalized symmetry** (humans and non-humans entered into the analysis in the same terms) are inherited verbatim. The [scallops case](Cases/scallops.md) is the canonical worked example.
- **Bruno Latour — classical ANT.** Latour, B. (1987). *Science in Action*; Latour, B. (1990). *Drawing Things Together*; Latour, B. (1991). *Technology is society made durable*; Latour, B. (2005). *Reassembling the Social*. The **programs / anti-programs of action**, the **mediator / intermediary** distinction (Latour 2005), **immutable mobiles** (1990), **inscriptions**, and the **black-boxing / punctualization** move all source here. `ant:ProgramOfAction`, `ant:AntiProgram`, `ant:Mediator`, `ant:Intermediary`, `ant:ImmutableMobile`, `ant:Inscription`, `ant:BlackBox` are the corresponding ontology terms. The [hotel-keys case](Cases/hotel-keys.md) reproduces Latour's 1991 demonstration of material delegation.
- **John Law — material semiotics and modes of ordering.** Law, J. (1986). *On the methods of long-distance control: vessels, navigation and the Portuguese route to India*; Law, J. (1994). *Organizing Modernity*; Law, J. (2008). *Actor Network Theory and Material Semiotics*. The Portuguese-ships immutable-mobile exemplar, the three forms of durability (`ant:MaterialDurability`, `ant:StrategicDurability`, `ant:DiscursiveStability`), and `ant:ModeOfOrdering` (Law 1994) all source here. **Law 2008 is the framing source for `ant-rdf`'s commitment that material-semiotics is a *toolkit / sensibility* rather than a *theory* (C1)** — and the source of the explicit reframing of "ANT" as one strand of broader material-semiotic work.
- **Annemarie Mol — multiplicity, enactment, ontological politics.** Mol, A. (2002). *The Body Multiple: Ontology in Medical Practice*; de Laet, M. & Mol, A. (2000). *The Zimbabwe Bush Pump: Mechanics of a Fluid Technology*; Mol, A. (1999). *Ontological Politics: A Word and Some Questions*. The §4.1.1 observer-relative `ant:Characterization` mechanism — where the same actant can be characterized as Mediator under one practice and Intermediary under another without OWL inconsistency — is the v1 partial down-payment on Mol's multiplicity. The **quad-readiness** the v1 file-system encodes (R8 / §4.5) is the migration path to named-graph multiplicity proper in v2. `ant:FluidObject` (deferred to v2) sources to de Laet & Mol's bush pump.
- **Allied threads.** Marilyn Strathern (1991, *Partial Connections*) on relations between multiples; Serres (1974) on translation as both *making-equivalent* and *shifting*; Akrich (1992) on de-scription of technical objects; Suchman (1987/2007) and Orr (1996) on situated practice — all carried as `dcterms:source` references on the relevant terms.
- **Published ANT-in-RDF prior art.** Goodwin, S. & Kuehn, E. (2021). *Latour's Hotel Keys: An Actor-Network Ontology* (NASKO vol. 8). The single published attempt at an ANT-in-RDF sketch before this project. `ant-rdf`'s [hotel-keys case](Cases/hotel-keys.md) reproduces their worked example using the synthesised vocabulary as a parity test.

These citations are not decorative. The [Concepts](Home.md) glossary in this wiki renders each term's `dcterms:source` as its "Source" section so a reader navigating the ontology can always trace a term back to the founding-text move it inherits.

### Technical lineage — a small pattern language

The docs-as-code paradigm this repo follows — versioned RDF as canonical source, deterministic compilers producing reviewable Markdown, an LLM-callable CLI as authoring surface, three-tier SHACL severity with waivers as recorded analytical decisions, an authoring contract for both humans and LLMs (`CLAUDE.md` / `SKILL.md`), and an ontologically-disciplined wiki for navigation — is a pattern that has been developed across several repositories before landing here. The technical lineage, in order of appearance:

1. **`trl-knowledgebase`** *(DSG-internal — referenced not linked)* is the original exemplar of the pattern (Zargham et al.) — Technology Readiness Level documentation as core OWL + SHACL shapes + Python CLI + instances split into per-initiative directories + compiled Markdown reports + a wiki for navigation. The structural pattern `ant-rdf` follows — `ontology/`, `instances/`, `src/`, `wiki/`, plus the `CLAUDE.md` / `SKILL.md` / `USER_GUIDE.md` authoring contracts — was first crystallised here.
2. **`RIME-product-docs`** *(DSG-internal — referenced not linked)* is the immediate template `ant-rdf` was scaffolded against — it inherits the pattern from `trl-knowledgebase` and sharpens conventions (tri-severity SHACL, deterministic-Turtle invariant, the verify/compile/wiki discipline that `ant verify` and `ant wiki` borrow directly).
3. **[Open-MBEE/flexo-conflict-resolution-policy-research](https://github.com/Open-MBEE/flexo-conflict-resolution-policy-research)** is a parallel research-side sibling (Zargham et al., 2026): not a docs-as-code instance itself, but the experiments in its `experiments/` directory — particularly the Exp 14–20 arc — empirically tested the git/RDF complementarity claim that §4 above inherits.

The first two are part of the Dynamical Systems Group's own AOI (per the theoretical-frame section above): DSG is *enacting* the theory by self-infrastructuring its operational knowledge through this pattern-family. `ant-rdf` is the publicly-shared iteration that extends the pattern beyond DSG's own operations to material-semiotic analysis of external assemblages.

The pattern is *replicating* across these repos because its ergonomics suit the kind of accountability that contemporary LLM-mediated work — being adopted widely and frequently mindlessly — has so far avoided. By forcing every claim through a versioned, validatable, reviewable artifact, the pattern adds rigour, reproducibility, and explainability without requiring the analyst to be sceptical of the LLM at every moment. We name this a small *pattern language* (in the Christopher Alexander sense) deliberately: the pattern is not specific to any one domain (TRL maturation, product management, conflict-resolution research, ANT/material-semiotic analysis), and each new repository it instantiates contributes back to the language's shared affordances. `ant-rdf` is the latest iteration; the comparison-study programme is, in part, a stress-test of whether the pattern itself holds up under sustained ethnographic use.
