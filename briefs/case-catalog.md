## hotel-keys

### The hotel-keys assemblage

<!-- https://w3id.org/ant/cases/hotel-keys/network -->

The network of hotel manager, guests, front-desk staff, key, weighted fob, and request sign analyzed by Latour (1991) in 'Technology is society made durable'. The fob translates the moral injunction 'please return your key' into a physical inconvenience that achieves what the polite sign alone cannot. Reproduces Goodwin & Kuehn (2021) 'Latour's Hotel Keys: An Actor-Network Ontology' (NASKO 8) using the synthesized ant-rdf vocabulary.

## scallops

### St Brieuc Bay scallop-farming network

<!-- https://w3id.org/ant/cases/scallops/network -->

The heterogeneous network Michel Callon analyzed in 'Some Elements of a Sociology of Translation' (1986). Three researchers from the Brest Oceanographic Centre attempt to enrol scallops, fishermen of St Brieuc Bay, and scientific colleagues into a programme to domesticate Pecten maximus and develop a commercial harvesting technique borrowed from Japanese practice. The translation succeeds for several seasons before fishermen trawl the protected larvae grounds and unravel the web.
